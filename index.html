Guide: The Art of Composting

Welcome to this month's GreenLife guide, where we'll delve into "The Art of Composting." Composting is a fantastic way to reduce waste, enrich your garden, and decrease your carbon footprint. In this step-by-step guide, we'll walk you through the process of starting your composting journey and explain why it's so beneficial for both you and the environment.

1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you begin, gather the following supplies:

  • Compost Bin or Pile: You can use a dedicated compost bin or create a compost pile in your backyard.
  • Green Materials: These are nitrogen-rich materials, such as fruit and vegetable scraps, coffee grounds, and grass clippings.
  • Brown Materials: These are carbon-rich materials, like dry leaves, straw, and shredded newspaper.
  • Water: You'll need water to keep your compost pile moist, similar to a wrung-out sponge.

Step 2: Choose a Location

Select a suitable location for your composting setup. It should be well-drained and receive partial sunlight. Avoid areas prone to extreme temperature fluctuations.

Step 3: Layer Your Materials

Begin by creating a layer of brown materials at the bottom of your compost bin or pile. This provides aeration and helps with drainage. Then, add a layer of green materials. Continue alternating layers of brown and green materials as you accumulate them.

Step 4: Maintain Moisture and Aeration

To ensure your compost breaks down efficiently, keep it as moist as a wrung-out sponge. Turn or aerate the pile regularly to provide oxygen for the decomposition process.

Step 5: Wait and Watch

Composting takes time, so be patient. Over the weeks and months, your compost will transform into rich, dark, earthy-smelling soil. You can test the readiness by grabbing a handful; it should crumble easily.

Why Composting Matters

Composting not only reduces the amount of organic waste in landfills but also produces nutrient-rich soil conditioner for your garden. It prevents the release of methane gas (a potent greenhouse gas) from decomposing organic matter in landfills. By composting, you're actively contributing to a healthier planet and promoting sustainable practices in your everyday life.

Welcome to the Eco-Sort Challenge!

Your mission is to sort items into the right bins - Compostable or Non-Compostable. To play, follow these steps:

  1. Drag and drop the items into the appropriate bins.
  2. Compostable items are eco-friendly and include things like food scraps, while non-compostable items are not biodegradable.
  3. If you make a mistake, don't worry! You can try again.

  • National Public Lands Day September 23, 2023

    • National Public Lands Day (NPLD) is an annual naational observance that typically falls on the fourth Saturday in September. It focuses on volunteer conservation of public lands. NPLD volunteers collect invasive plants, build and maintain trails, plant trees, shrubs and other plants, remove trash from trails and other places. They also donate money through volunteer services to help improve public land sites. Visit the nps.gov website to check out a list of volunteer events.

  • America Recycles Day November 15, 2023

    • America Recycles Day (ARD) is the only nationally-recognized day dedicated to promoting and celebrating recycling in the United States. ARD educates and encourages individuals on how to properly recycle and to pledge to recycle more in their everyday lives. Vist the Keep America Beautiful website to volunteer, find a project near you, donate, or just take the pledge.

  • World Water Day March 22, 2024

    • World Water Day is about accelerating change to solve the water and sanitation crisis. If everyone commits to making a change in the way they use, consume, and manage water in their lives, they can make a difference. Get your kit and spread the word at worldwaterday.org.

  • Earth Day April 22, 2024

    • First celebrated in 1970, Earth Day has mobilized million of Americans from all walks of life to birth the modern environmental movement. Earth Day has evolved into the largest civic event on Earth, activating the masses to safeguard our planet. Head to earthday.org to find or register your own events for this special day and spark a change in your community.

List of Green Companies

Embrace a greener lifestyle by supporting businesses committed to sustainability! Opting for products from companies dedicated to eco-friendly practices, such as those offering 100% biodegradable products and utilizing carbon offsets, empowers you to make environmentally conscious choices. By patronizing these eco-champions, you contribute to the collective effort of reducing environmental impact and fostering a healthier planet. Your support not only encourages responsible business practices but also ensures a brighter, more sustainable future for generations to come. Join the movement toward greener living and support businesses that prioritize the well-being of both consumers and the Earth.

  1. Nike

    2. Nike is an American multinational corporation that manufactures and sells footwear, apparel, sports equipment, and accessories. Apart from showcasing the importance of green initiatives through advertising, Nike is a leader in putting green ideas into practice.

  2. Apple

    3. Apple, a company based in Cupertino, CA focuses on three aspects of sustainability — minimizing the impact on climate, preserving natural resources, and using safer materials in its products and work environment. As a part of that commitment, Apple has created several wind and solar farms that power their data centers, retail stores, and corporate offices. The corporation is also actively preserving materials through increased production efficiency, renewable resources, and designing durable products that last longer.

  3. Patagonia

    4. If you’re an outdoorsy type, you must’ve heard of Patagonia, one of the world’s most successful retailers of activewear. Since outdoorsy folks are usually very eco-conscious, it should surprise that Patagonia’s corporate philosophy is green to the core. They have invested in repair centers around the world to increase the longevity of their products and lower the carbon footprint that goes with manufacturing new items.

  4. Starbucks

    5. As the world’s largest coffeehouse chain and a symbol of the United States’ second wave of coffee culture, Starbucks has developed an eco-friendly cup that doubles the recycled content and reusability. This green company is also known for buying 100% ethically-sourced coffee and making coffee the first globally sustainable agricultural product. Starbucks has also pledged to provide 100 million healthy coffee trees to farmers whose plantations have suffered because of disease and climate change.

  5. IKEA

    6. This Swedish company sources almost 50% of its wood from sustainably harvested forests and 100% of its cotton from growers that meet the Better Cotton standards. These standards propose reduced energy and water use, as well as regulate the use of fertilizers and pesticides. IKEA uses more than 700,000 solar panels that power its stores around the world and has announced its goal to be powered by 100% renewable energy.

  6. Seventh Generation

    7. Seventh Generation is a company that makes personal care and cleaning products that started its green journey in 1988. From the moment it was founded, the company’s mission became “to transform the world into a healthy, sustainable & equitable place for the next seven generations.” Realizing that packaging used for selling and shipping products is a huge source of waste, Seventh Generation uses a packaging solution that is just as environmentally friendly as their plant-based products made of sustainably sourced ingredients.

  7. A Good Company

    8. For A Good Company, being green means more than just doing no harm to the planet. Its founder Anders Ankarlid is determined to do everything he can to fight climate change, which reflects in every product his company creates. Their lineup includes products like the world’s first climate-positive notebook made with stone paper and luxury pens made of melted down illegal firearms from El Salvador.

  8. Honda

    9. In 2004, Honda began its Tree Fund Program to curb growing carbon emissions. Since its start, more than 700,000 trees were planted within the program across the New Zealand landscape. One of its goals is to reduce CO2 emissions by 30% for motorcycles, automobiles, and power products, compared with those manufactured between 2000-2020. Honda has entered a long-term virtual power purchase agreement (PPA) for renewable wind and solar, which will cover more than 60% of the company’s energy needs in North America.

  9. Disney

    10. Just as in the animation and film industry, Disney is a giant when it comes to energy-efficiency. The company uses zero net direct greenhouse gas policies in all of its facilities. Disney is also actively working to reduce electrical and water use, which reduces indirect greenhouse emissions. The company also commits to produce or purchase 100% zero-carbon electricity for its global operations by 2030.

  10. New Belgium Brewing Company

    11. New Belgium Brewing is among the largest craft beer companies in the US and an advocate of environmental protection initiatives since its founding in 1991. Based in Fort Collins, Colorado, the brewery is determined to work only with sustainable vendors and holds a Platinum-certified Zero Waste Business certificate, which means 99.9% of their waste is disposed of through a recycling program.
